1  Preferences

Economists uses the preference relation to capture the ordering that an agent gives to alternatives between which they might choose:

  • Strong (or strict) preference (\succ): “is better than”

  • Weak preference (\succcurlyeq): “is at least as good as”

  • Indifference (~): “is as good as” or “the agent is indifferent between”. x \sim y if and only if x\succcurlyeq y and y\succcurlyeq x.

I might have the following preference:

  • banana \succ apples

I strongly prefer bananas to apples.

Similarly:

  • bananas \sim oranges

I am indifferent between bananas and oranges.
