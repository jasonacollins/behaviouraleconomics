# What is behavioural economics? {.unnumbered}

Behavioural economics is a discipline that seeks to increase the explanatory power of traditional economic approaches by incorporating more realistic psychological foundations.

Behavioural economics retains the general framework and tools used by economists, such as utility maximisation, but deviates from some assumptions to generate new insights and better predictions. These deviations are generally grounded in observed behaviour rather than abstract principles.

As behavioural economics revisits our fundamental understanding of economic behaviour, it has implications for all areas of economics.

## What is not behavioural economics?

The term "behavioural economics" is commonly used to describe work that is better described as psychology or "behavioural science".

Economics is the study of how economic agents make decisions under conditions of scarcity and of the interactions between those agents.

Behavioural science is the study of human behaviour. Behavioural economics is not just about studying human behaviour, but about how a better understanding of human behaviour could improve the study of the decisions of economic agents and their interactions.

Similarly, psychology is the study of the human mind, decisions and behaviour. It is part of the behavioural sciences. Psychology is broad with many discrete fields. Behavioural economics tends to draw on a small subset of psychological research in refining the economic view on human behaviour.
